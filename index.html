Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any prior aviation experience to start?
Not at all! The majority of our students come to us with absolute zero flight experience. We build your knowledge from the ground up starting with the basics. If you're unsure if flying is right for you, we highly recommend booking a Discovery Flight. This allows you to get a hands-on experience in the cockpit alongside a certified instructor before committing to a full program.
How long does it take to get a Private Pilot License (PPL)?
The FAA requires a minimum of 40 flight hours, but the national average to complete the training is closer to 55-65 hours. If you fly consistently (about 2 to 3 times a week) and study your ground school materials, you can typically complete your PPL in 3 to 6 months. Your dedication and weather conditions in Los Angeles play a big role in the timeline.
What are the exact age requirements for flight training?
You can begin taking flight lessons at any age (we have students starting in their early teens!). However, federal regulations require you to be at least 16 years old to fly an airplane solo (by yourself), and you must be 17 years old to take your final practical exam (checkride) and receive your Private Pilot Certificate.
Do I need 20/20 perfect vision to become a pilot?
No, perfect uncorrected vision is not required. As long as your vision can be corrected to 20/40 or better (using prescription glasses or contact lenses) for near and distant vision, you can successfully pass the FAA Class 3 Medical examination and fly safely. Color blindness may impose some night flying restrictions, but you can still get licensed.
What is an FAA Medical Certificate and how do I get one?
An FAA Medical Certificate is an official document ensuring you meet the health and fitness standards required to safely operate an aircraft. The examination is conducted by an FAA-designated Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). It involves a basic physical, vision, and hearing test. We guide all our enrolled students on how to find an AME locally in LA and complete the MedXPress application.
Are your training programs and fleet officially FAA-approved?
Absolutely. All our flight training programs, syllabi, and instructors operate in strict adherence to the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) regulations under Part 61. Our partner fleet undergoes rigorous mandatory 100-hour and annual inspections by certified mechanics to ensure 100% compliance and safety for our students.
