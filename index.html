Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any prior aviation experience to start?
Not at all! The majority of our students come to us with absolute zero flight experience. We build your knowledge from the ground up starting with the basics. If you're unsure if flying is right for you, we highly recommend booking a Discovery Flight to get hands-on experience.
How long does it take to get a Private Pilot License (PPL)?
The FAA requires a minimum of 40 flight hours, but the national average is closer to 55-65 hours. If you fly consistently (about 2 to 3 times a week), you can typically complete your PPL in 3 to 6 months. Your dedication and weather conditions play a big role.
What are the exact age requirements for flight training?
You can begin taking flight lessons at any age. However, federal regulations require you to be at least 16 years old to fly an airplane solo, and you must be 17 years old to take your final practical exam (checkride).
Do I need 20/20 perfect vision to become a pilot?
No, perfect uncorrected vision is not required. As long as your vision can be corrected to 20/40 or better (using prescription glasses or contact lenses), you can successfully pass the FAA Class 3 Medical examination and fly safely.
What is an FAA Medical Certificate and how do I get one?
An FAA Medical Certificate ensures you meet the health standards to safely operate an aircraft. It is conducted by an FAA-designated Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). We guide all our enrolled students on how to find an AME locally in LA.
Are your training programs and fleet officially FAA-approved?
Absolutely. All our flight training programs, syllabi, and instructors operate in strict adherence to the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) regulations under Part 61. Our partner fleet undergoes rigorous mandatory inspections.
