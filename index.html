Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any prior aviation experience to start?
Not at all! The majority of our students come to us with absolute zero flight experience. We build your knowledge from the ground up starting with the basics.
How long does it take to get a Private Pilot License (PPL)?
The FAA requires a minimum of 40 flight hours. If you fly consistently (about 2 to 3 times a week), you can typically complete your PPL in 3 to 6 months.
What are the exact age requirements for flight training?
You can begin taking lessons at any age. Federal regulations require you to be at least 16 years old to fly solo, and 17 years old to receive your License.
Do I need 20/20 perfect vision to become a pilot?
No. As long as your vision can be corrected to 20/40 or better (using prescription glasses or contact lenses), you can successfully fly safely.
What is an FAA Medical Certificate and how do I get one?
It ensures you meet health standards. It is conducted by an FAA-designated Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). We guide all enrolled students on how to find one locally.
Are your training programs and fleet officially FAA-approved?
Absolutely. All programs and instructors operate in strict adherence to FAA regulations under Part 61.
